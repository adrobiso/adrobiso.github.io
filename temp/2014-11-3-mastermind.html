Mastermind on a FPGA

In my digital design class, which worked with an FPGA on a Diligent Nexys 2 board, I chose to recreate the board game Mastermind as my final project. I changed the rules slightly to work around the fact that I only had binary input, but the core of the game is still the same.

The first change was that rather than picking four knobs of six different colors, the first player used eight switches to create the target combination. They would then press a button to save the combination, and put the switches in a random configuration. The second player would then put the switches into their first guess, and press a second button. This would update the seven-segment display, telling them how many switches were in the wrong state (but not which ones) as well as tell them how many attempts they had made. This would continue until they guessed the right combination.
